## 🎨 Changing skin settings

Every skin has options to adjust its behavior. 

1. Click on any active skin on your desktop.
2. Right-click the skin to open the context menu.
3. Select Settings to change the position or transparency.
4. Select "Stay on screen" if you want the item to remain visible at all times.
5. Use "Draggable" to move the item with your mouse. Drag it to a new location and uncheck the box to lock it in place.
6. Adjust "Transparency" to make the skin blend into your wallpaper.

## ⚠️ Troubleshooting common issues

If you encounter problems, check these items.

### The program does not start
Restart your computer. Sometimes system files lock during an update. A restart clears these locks and allows the program to initialize correctly.

### Skins do not update information
Some widgets rely on external websites for data. Check your internet connection. If the connection works, wait a few minutes. Servers for weather or currency data occasionally go offline for maintenance.

### High CPU usage
If your computer slows down, unload some skins. Some complex skins use more processing power than others. Open the Manage window and unload skins that you do not use often.

### Icons look blurry
Right-click your desktop and select Display settings. Ensure your Scale and layout settings match the recommended values for your monitor. 

## 📖 Glossary of terms

*   Skin: A widget or desktop element that displays information or provides a function.
*   Manage window: The central control panel where you load and unload skins.
*   .rmskin: The file format for skin packages. This allows you to install multiple files at once.
*   Tray icon: The small icon in the taskbar near the clock that provides quick access to the program.
*   Load: The action of making a skin visible on your screen.
*   Unload: The action of closing a skin and removing it from your desktop.
*   Settings: The configuration menu for specific skins. 
*   Transparency: The level of see-through for a skin. 100% means the skin is invisible. 0% means it is fully solid. 
*   Draggable: A setting that allows you to click and move a skin with your mouse cursor.
*   Refresh: A command that reloads the skin files to show the latest changes. Use this after you edit a skin file.
